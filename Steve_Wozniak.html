Steve Wozniak

Dates: Born on August 11, 1950

Significant Contribution

The “Steves” likely require no introduction, but they started Apple Computer in 1976. Their first computer was built by Wozniak and sold by Jobs. The Apple II was the machine that really launched Apple Computer into the mainstream. Jobs was kicked out of Apple in 1985. He started a new company NeXT Inc. and Pixar. In 1997 he returned as the CEO and remained there until he died in 2011. Wozniak also left Apple in 1985. He earned his teaching degree online under an assumed name and taught high school and middle school for several years before returning to technology as a speaker and writer.

Biography

Steve Wozniak, born on August 11, 1950, in San Jose, California, is an American inventor, engineer, and computer programmer who co-founded Apple Inc. His passion for technology and electronics emerged at an early age, and he developed a keen interest in designing and building electronic gadgets. Wozniak's groundbreaking contribution to the tech industry came in 1976 when he, along with Steve Jobs and Ronald Wayne, founded Apple Computer (now Apple Inc.). Wozniak played a pivotal role in the creation of the Apple I and Apple II computers, which marked the onset of the personal computer revolution. His innovative designs and engineering prowess revolutionized the computing landscape, setting the stage for Apple's immense success. Despite his significant contributions, Wozniak left Apple in 1985 to pursue other ventures and focus on his passion for education. Beyond his accomplishments in the tech realm, Wozniak is also known for his philanthropy, supporting various educational initiatives and STEM (science, technology, engineering, and mathematics) programs. His legacy as a pioneer in the world of personal computing and technology continues to inspire future generations of innovators and entrepreneurs.
